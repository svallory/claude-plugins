# Book-Specific Learned Patterns

Patterns that apply specifically to book chapters (technical books, non-fiction, long-form). Updated by the ai-engineer during humanization rounds.

---

### [PROVEN] Smooth Paragraph and Section Transitions

**Rule:** Use varied transition techniques instead of mechanical temporal markers:
1. **Causal bridges:** Connect paragraphs through cause-effect rather than chronology
2. **Thematic echoes:** End one paragraph with a concept that the next paragraph picks up
3. **Question-answer pairs:** End with an implicit question, start with the answer
4. **Contrast pivots:** Use contrast to create natural momentum
5. **Narrative continuation:** Let the story flow without announcing time jumps
6. **AVOID:** "And then," "Next," "Subsequently," "Following this," as paragraph openers

---

### [PROVEN] Breaking Up Statistics-Dense Sections with Narrative

**Rule:** For every 2-3 statistics or data points, include one narrative element:
1. **Human reaction or consequence:** "When those numbers hit the briefing room, the reaction was silence."
2. **Concrete scene or moment:** "Picture a bank security officer in Bangladesh, staring at transaction logs that made no sense."
3. **Personal voice/opinion:** "I find that number staggering. Think about it for a moment."
4. **Analogy or comparison:** "That's roughly the GDP of a small country, moved in a weekend."
5. **Rhetorical pause:** "Let that sink in." / "Read that again."
