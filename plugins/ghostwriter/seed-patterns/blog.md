# Blog-Specific Learned Patterns

Patterns that apply primarily to blog posts and casual writing. Updated by the ai-engineer during humanization rounds.

*For book chapters, see `book.md` and `global.md` — the patterns there take precedence.*

---

### Topic-Level AI Saturation Signal (Critical)

On AI-favorite topics (productivity, self-help, morning routines, meditation, work-life balance, generic how-tos), differentiate by questioning the premise, self-undermining, cultural pushback, unresolved tension, anti-advice framing, and personal contradiction.

---

### Advice vs. Processing Frame (Critical)

Reframe away from advice: "Here's what I tried and I'm still not sure" (processing frame) not "Here's what works" (advice frame). Use "I" more than "you." Avoid imperatives.

---

### Earnestness as AI Signal on Self-Help Topics (High)

Add skeptical distance: eye-rolling at the genre, mocking the earnestness, self-deprecation about engaging with self-help, questioning whether caring is good.

---

### Essay vs. Listicle Structure on Saturated Topics (High)

Lean toward essay structure: avoid clear sections, meander between ideas, end without closing the loop, write like thinking out loud. AVOID exactly 3 points/tips/items. Prefer 2, 4, 5, or 7 items. Avoid numbered lists in blog posts; prefer prose or unstructured bullets.

---

### AI Vocabulary Clustering (Critical)

NEVER use "delves" in blog writing. NEVER cluster AI-trigger words in the same paragraph. Maximum 1 high-risk word per 3 paragraphs.

---

### Formulaic Opening/Closing Patterns (Critical)

NEVER use "In today's [X] world/age/landscape." NEVER use "In conclusion" in blog writing. Start with specifics or mid-thought. End naturally.

---

### Hedging Patterns (High)

Remove academic hedging ("can significantly enhance" → "boosts"). When hedging remains, cluster it irregularly — multiple hedges in one section, none in another. Let hedging reflect actual moments of uncertainty, not systematic caution.

---

### Vulnerability-Claim Rhythm Imbalance (High)

Break alternating vulnerability/claim patterns. Allow back-to-back vulnerabilities or back-to-back confident assertions. Uneven rhythm is human.

---

### Structural Variation and Asymmetry (High)

Introduce structural asymmetry: spend 3x more words on what matters most; let one section be notably shorter or longer. Break formulaic paragraph structures. Scatter content. Start with failure stories. Interrupt lists with asides. End abruptly without CTA. Vary space per point. Mix short punchy sentences with longer flowing ones. Use one-sentence paragraphs for emphasis.

---

### Authentic Messiness and Tangents (High)

Include 1-2 genuine tangents per 500 words that survived editing because the writer liked them. Add genuinely irrelevant details that don't serve the narrative. Allow sentences to trail off. Include thoughts that go nowhere. Add mid-paragraph fragments. Add self-corrections mid-paragraph. Use rough transitions ("Anyway," "So yeah"). Let at least one paragraph ramble. Not every tangent needs resolution.

---

### Sentence Burstiness and Perplexity (High)

Aggressive sentence length variation is the most powerful human signal. Mix short punchy sentences with longer narrative ones naturally. Increase perplexity by varying sentence structures aggressively, using occasional fragments, and choosing unexpected but natural word combinations.

---

### Specificity Over Generics (Critical)

Specificity beats all other rules. Include concrete, verifiable details: exact times, personal struggles, specific names, measurable outcomes, app names, book recommendations, cultural references. NEVER use "Research has consistently demonstrated..." or "Studies show that..." (vague plural). Cite specifics or skip citations entirely.

---

### Style Constraint Adherence (Critical)

INTERNALIZE context parameters as hard requirements. Reread tone and voice requirements before writing. Match them exactly.

---

### Filler Phrase Elimination (Moderate)

NEVER use "It's worth noting that..." / "It's important to note that..." / "It should be mentioned that..." Cut and rewrite without filler.
